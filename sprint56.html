Sprint 5 & 6

Sprint 5


What was done?

This was our final sprint before presenting our final production website.

This sprint was about bettering our users experience, fixing any bugs we found while testing functionality, and increasing security.

What I did

During this sprint, I chose to work on increasing the security of our website by methods of sanitizing user input.

I also added required fields, and added code where if a form was incorrectly filled out, old input was shown in the redirected page.

Other smaller tasks included transversing our webpage, fixing any typos and making things more simplistic where possible.

Technical achievements

One technical challenge I had during this sprint, was having a git error when migrating my databases, because of a change another team member had made. To overcome this challenge, I went and asked the member who made the change, who advised me I simply needed to add a dependancy to my composer file and update composer.

This demonstrated good communication to quickly and easily overcome an issue.

Was I agile? How? How not?

During this sprint, I feel like I was agile.

I worked at a good, steady pace, I worked to my strengths to benefit the team, while taking the time to go above and beyond, testing features of the website.

I did some tasks that were beyond my scope as per tasks that we given to us, and I also communicated different UI design ideas to the member of the team that was working on them, that I felt would make our website more usable.

I accomplished all my tasks this sprint, testing my features, and felt the outcome was good.

At the end of this sprint, we had a full, working production copy of our website, inclusive off all functionality that was asked, while keeping usablility and security in mind.

Sprint conclusion

During this sprint, I was agile, working well with my team, asking for help, offering help, offering ideas and taking in advice from others.

The sprint went very smoothly, the team worked at a good pace, communication was good throughout, and tasks were finished on time.

I took note of how I worked during my last sprint and tried to work the same, communicating well, taking on a good workload, taking on challenges, producing what our customer wanted, and making sure it functioned.

Conclusion

Over the period of 5 sprints, I learnt what the principles of agile were and what it meant to be agile.

In the beginning, I wasn't an agile worker, but towards the end, particularly sprint 4 and 5, I felt I was agile, following many agile principles, displaying the traits of a good team member.

In the beginning, my communication was not very good. I was not confident, did not want to take on challenges, and didn't care much for working software, as long as I was getting work done.

In the end, I communicated regularly, and found regular stand up meetings was a good way of working with the team. I also communicated with team members using Microsoft Teams, as well as in and inbetween other classes.

I took on a much larger workload towards the end. I did lots of self directed learning, particularly about Laravel and MVC models and how they interacted. This helped me get tasks done as it was the base of our website.

I learnt more about databases and how they worked and I took on challenges which required me to do self-directed learning to achieve them. The pressure of that, along with the pressure of helping the team made me work harder to accomplish tasks.

In the beginning, I feel I wouldn't achieve what I had achieved by the end of sprint 5. Learning agile principles and being agile helped me achieve these.
