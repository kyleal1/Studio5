Sprint 1 & 2

Sprint 1

This was our first sprint where we were introduced to our project and the expectations of our project.

What was done?

For this sprint, we were required to start a web application.

We were to add basic functionality such as registering an account, adding a game and viewing games that had been added

What I did

During this sprint, I did basic things like creating views, editing HTML so we could view content, and adding functions to our controllers, like a create and store function for our games controller.

Due to the nature of our team and the lack of natural English speakers, I stood up and helped write all our User Stories for this sprint. With me being a natural English speaker, it made this proccess quicker and more efficient.

I also needed to fix a database MYSQL error, regarding a default string length.

During this sprint, our team had 4 stand up meetings, I attended all of these.

Personal achievements

During this Sprint, I was in the very beggining stages of learning Laravel. I knew nothing about how the MVC model worked.

Through my own research online, I was able to get a better understanding of Laravel, particularly models, controllers and functions.

When I couldn't figure something out and I couldn't find it online, I would ask our scrum master if he was able to help and provide guidance.

Technical achievements

During this sprint, one team member added a lot of temporary files, deleting our .env and .gitignore file.

With the help of Elise and the other team members, we were able to figure where the issue went wrong, and worked out how to fix it.

I was not really part of fixing the issue, but I worked with my team during this issue, providing advice, listening and learning on the way.

This showed me the importance of working with a team through issues, and I learnt how to revert changes and I learnt what importance the .env and .gitignore files had.

Was I agile? How? How not?

During this sprint, I feel I showed some principles of being agile, but overall, I was not agile.

I was agile in the way I worked with the team. We had stand-up meetings, although not as many as we should have.

We produced software with basic functionality, it was working and we achieved our goals.

We did not regularly reflect on our work, only during the end of our sprint.

I feel I could have taken on more technically challenging work, and more of it. Being inexperienced at Laravel, I was scared of taking the risk of doing more challenging tasks as I didn't want to make our team fall behind by attempting to do more than I was capable of at the time.

I didn't work at a continuous pace. Some days I would do more work, some days less, some days none at all.

Sprint conclusion

During this sprint, I was not agile, though me and my team showed understanding of some agile principles.

I felt I was valuable to my team in that I created User Stories and completed work that I undertook.

I feel I could have been more valuable by taking on more challenges.

Our Sprint Restrospective showed that we needed to have more scrum meetings, communicate better and divide tasks based on what people were good at.

During this sprint, I learnt about what it means to be agile. I learnt more about Laravel and Git, how they function and how to use them together.

I learnt how we should be working as a team, what team work can achieve, and learnt how to be more efficient as a team member for the future.

Sprint 2

What was done?

For this sprint, we added more functionality to our website, and also made so design changes.

We were to add searching of games to our website, add game details which could be viewed from our list of games, and make a more responsive, better looking website.

This was also the first time we met face to face with our client, Hymie, who was able to describe in more depth to us, what he was after.

What I did

During this sprint I was put in charge of design of the website, adding navigation and adding a colour scheme.

This was not a very challenging job, but one I felt comfortable doing. Looking back, I definitely could have taken on something more challenging.

During this sprint, our team increased stand up meetings from 4 to 6. This was an improvement, and improved our communication, but we still felt we needed more.

Personal achievements

During this sprint, we had our first chance to communicate with our client.

During some times, I found communicating with our client difficult. I tried communicating with the client regarding an appropriate colour scheme, and in response, got a link to a website with 50 nice colour schemes.

While this was good, it didn't help me in knowing what the client was after.

I had to be persistant in asking what colour schemes the client was interested in, while proposing ideas.

After some back and forwards conversation, we were able to decide on a colour scheme, while sizing, design decisions, fonts etc were left up to me to decide.

I found it difficult to communicate with the client during this time, sometimes not hearing back for a few days, but being persistant in conjunction with my own research, we were able to decide on an appropriate colour scheme.

Technical achievements

During this sprint,I feel there wasn't many technical achievements or difficulties I came across.

This is mainly due to me not taking on a very technically challenging task, and keeping well within my comfort zone

I did have the challenge of working with such a big Bootstrap css file, this made finding styling elements I wanted to change difficult. I needed to search a lot and change elements and revert changes until I found it changed what I wanted.

Was I agile? How? How not?

During this sprint, I feel I showed some principles of being agile, but overall, I was not agile.

I did not take on challenging enough tasks where I felt satisfied I had helped the team enough.

As a team, our communication was better across the board, using the platform Microsoft Teams to communicate within our group and to our client.

We increased stand up meetings, which helped us know where we all were, and if anyone needed help.

When we had our sprint meeting with our client, although he was happy with my design, he felt it could be improved, which I worked on during the next sprint. This can fall within the agile principle of working software. Although the client was happy with what I produced, it could have been better.

Overall, I didn't feel like I was agile during this sprint. I need to increase my workload and technical challenges, but I felt I communicated well to my team and to the client.

Sprint conclusion

During this sprint, I was not agile, though me and my team showed understanding of some agile principles.

As a whole, our team didn't take on many challenging tasks during this sprint, but I found that I especially didn't.

I took on the role of doing design as I felt it was easy and not challenging. At this stage, I was still reluctant to challenge myself and take on a difficult challenge.

Our communication as a team improved this sprint, with more stand up meetings, and more communication using Microsoft Teams.

Our sprint meeting with our client went well, he was happy with what we produced, which was working software, although he did note my design could be better.

During this sprint, I realised the effectiveness of good communication. It was easier to communicate, and easier to know where everyone was in terms of progress and what was expected of us.

We found the amount of stand up meetings we had was good, an improvement on last sprint, but still we could do more which could benefit us.

I started to realise from this sprint, that I needed to take on more challenging tasks to be an effective team member. I started realising others were doing more than me, also in a technical aspect, and I needed to take on the challenge to feel more satisfied.
